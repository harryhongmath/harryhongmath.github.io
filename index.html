Philosophy

Over the past couple of years, I've come to recognize that the online communication is the nature of the future and started realigning my fundamentals. As I'm assuming responsibility for the protection of this rapidly changing climate, I decided to build the identity from the scratch that'll not only reinforce my existing reputation but also reconstruct my new main target: more communication; this new brand identity is both inward- and outward-facing.

This Identity has two main goals to achieve:

To give strong, consistent, and attentive feeling. It is vital to convey these feeling in order to give clients more reassurance and warmth.

To focus more on the new means of communication: digital. I believe face-to-face or verbal communication should be the best but it may be unrealistic at times. The digital form of communication, especially emails, is now the most preferred method of communication, according to a SendGrind study called The Future of Digital Communication. Under this new Identity, I decided to discard all existing print-based formats such as PDFs, and design new digital formats that can be readily accessible in any devices, especially focusing more on viewing experience on mobile phones.

Logo

The construction and composition of the logo is based on a combination of two geometric shapes: a circle and an equilateral triangle. An integral sign, symbolizing limitless, is inscribed in the lemniscate, symbolizing infinity, which is inscribed in a circle, symbolizing perfection, which is inscribed in an equilateral triangle, symbolizing unity and success. The triangle and its incircle are not parts of the final image but the construction and its white space yield an upward movement and stable formation.

An alternate logo with different color combination with reversed black and white is available to use on dark background only.

The imaginary rectangle that circumscribes the logo is to be surrounded at minimum distance equal to the radius of one of the circles that compose the lemniscate. No visual elements are allowed within this range.

The minimum onscreen size of the logo is 150 pixels wide. The minimum print size is to be determined.

Color

The three primary colors are used: pure white, blue, and pure black. The shade of the blue is chosen to give viewers a sense of trustworthiness and stability. Its Pantone color is estimated. Some other saturated colors may be used only as secondary accent colors.

In most types of documents and forms, pure black text on pure white background is to be used. However, when "dark mode" is turned on the device, slightly darker white text (opacity of 87%, 60%, 38% for high emphasis, medium emphasis, disabled, respectively) on dark grey background (HEX #212121) is to be used if possible. Otherwise, all text/background color combinations are to be chosen according to the Web Content Accessibility Guidlines (WCAG 2.0) level AA, requiring a 4.5:1 color contrast between text and background for normal text, and 3:1 for large text.

Typography

The Identity primarily use the Source Sans Pro family in digital publications whenever possible. This is prefered over the Open Sans family due to: a) its clearer distinction between the uppercase "I" and the lowercase "L", b) warmer and more imperfect (therefore more natural) feeling, and c) relatively easier to read in smaller sizes.

If the Source Sans Pro is not available, the Open Sans family may be used. This family may feel more aesthetically pleasing for its simpler, colder, and emotionless look, such as the straight tail of the uppercase "Q" and decending uppercase "J" below the baseline.

Where web fonts are not available such as emails, Arial, the most widely accepted typeface family, may substitute. The typeface families for printed publications are to be determined. Other details of typography other than the typeface such as sizes, kernings, leadings, line spacings, etc, are to be determined.
